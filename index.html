About Me

I’m Coach Xavier, and my passion for swimming began in childhood when my friends and I would walk long distances just to reach a pool, despite not knowing how to swim. But one day, something happened that changed my perspective forever. My friends and I, excited for another day of fun, decided to venture into the deep end of the pool. None of us had any real training, but we felt invincible — kids often do. As I swam farther from the shallow side, the water began to feel different. I struggled to stay afloat, and for the first time, I panicked. My arms flailed, my breathing quickened, and everything around me seemed to blur. I was scared and helpless.

Luckily, a lifeguard noticed me struggling and acted swiftly. In moments, I was pulled from the water, shaken but safe. That day, I realized that swimming wasn’t just about fun — it was about respect for the water and understanding its dangers. My love for swimming remained, but now, safety became my top priority.

Years later, that experience shaped my career when I became a lifeguard supervisor at a water park in Dubai. Through my training, I learned how essential it is to never underestimate the power of water, no matter how confident you feel. I’ve seen firsthand how quickly things can go wrong when swimmers don’t respect the safety rules or overestimate their abilities.

Now, as a coach, I teach my students the importance of building skills in the water while always keeping safety in mind. Whether you're learning to swim for the first time or refining your strokes, understanding the risks and preparing for them is just as important as having fun. A confident swimmer is one who not only enjoys the water but also knows how to navigate it safely.

Let’s work together to make sure that your swimming journey is as safe as it is exciting. Safety isn’t just a lesson — it’s a skill that can save lives.
